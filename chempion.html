Redesigning a Jupyter notebook-based chemical inventory system into a data-dense, production-grade lab management dashboard.

The Problem

A tool built for one person
used by an entire lab.

ChemPion started as a Jupyter notebook system for tracking chemical inventory. Functional for a developer, but inaccessible for lab staff who needed to check stock levels at a glance without running Python. The data was all there. The interface just wasn't designed for humans.

01
No at-a-glance overview. Checking stock required running a cell and reading raw output. Critical alerts were buried in code.
02
No visual hierarchy. All data was treated equally — a chemical running out looked identical to one with years of shelf life.
03
No actions from the interface. Adding a chemical, recording a dispense, or exporting required editing code directly.

Design Thinking

Three decisions that shaped the redesign.

01

Status as a first-class citizen

Every chemical has a status — in stock, expiring soon, or low/out of stock. In the original system, this required reading a date and mentally computing urgency.

Color-coded pills communicate urgency before you read a single word — matching how lab staff actually scan a shelf.
02

Overview before detail

The dashboard opens on four KPI cards — total chemicals, total value, expiring soon, low stock. The numbers that matter most are visible immediately.

Most inventory checks are "is anything wrong?" The dashboard answers that in under a second, then lets you drill down if needed.
03

Actions in the interface

Adding a chemical, filtering by status, searching, exporting to CSV — all done through the UI without touching code.

A tool is only useful if the people who need it can actually use it. Separating the interface from the implementation means lab staff can work independently.

Result

From notebook to dashboard.

The redesigned ChemPion makes the same data accessible to everyone in the lab — not just the person who wrote the code. Five fully functional sections, real-time filtering and search, and form-based workflows for all common actions.
